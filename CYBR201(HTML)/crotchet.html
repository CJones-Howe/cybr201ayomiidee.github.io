Common Stitch Types

Mastering these foundational stitches will allow you to read patterns, design your own, and create textured, beautiful pieces.

  • Chain Stitch (ch): The starting point for most crochet projects.
  • Single Crochet (sc): Dense and tight‚Äîperfect for toys and pot holders.
  • Half Double Crochet (hdc): Slightly taller than single crochet with more drape.
  • Double Crochet (dc): Taller stitch, often used in scarves and blankets.
  • Treble Crochet (tr): Even taller‚Äîused in lacy and open designs.
  • Slip Stitch (sl st): Used for joining and creating seamless edges.

Specialty Stitches:

  • Bobble Stitch: Adds 3D texture‚Äîgreat for blankets and pillows.
  • Shell Stitch: Fans of stitches forming a scalloped effect.
  • V-Stitch: Combines two double crochets‚Äîgood for airy projects.
  • Granny Square: Classic building block for larger projects.

All About Crochet Hooks

The right hook makes all the difference. Consider material, grip, and size when choosing yours.

Hook Materials:

  • Aluminum: Smooth and affordable‚Äîgreat for beginners.
  • Wood/Bamboo: Lightweight with more friction‚Äîideal for slippery yarns.
  • Plastic: Lightweight and inexpensive, but can warp over time.
  • Steel: Used for tiny lace and thread work.

Hook Sizes:

Sizes range from 0.6mm (for lace) to 15mm (for bulky yarn). US sizes go from B-1 (2.25mm) to Q (15mm).

Ergonomics:

Choose soft-grip or padded hooks if crocheting for long periods. Brands like Clover Amour and Tulip Etimo are highly rated.

Choosing the Right Yarn

Yarn determines texture, durability, and color. Each project might need a different yarn type!

Fiber Types:

  • Cotton: Great for summer wear, dishcloths, and amigurumi.
  • Acrylic: Inexpensive, washable, and available in all colors and weights.
  • Wool: Warm, breathable, and stretchy‚Äîideal for garments and winter wear.
  • Blends: Mix benefits (like cotton-acrylic or wool-silk) for comfort and performance.

Yarn Weights:

  • Lace: Very fine; used for doilies and shawls.
  • Sport/DK: Lighter weight for baby items and garments.
  • Worsted: Medium weight‚Äîperfect for most patterns.
  • Bulky/Super Bulky: Thick, cozy yarns great for quick blankets and hats.

Yarn Labels:

Always read the label for hook size suggestions, washing instructions, and yardage per skein.

Granny Square Pattern

The classic granny square is a foundational motif in crochet. It‚Äôs beginner-friendly and endlessly customizable, perfect for blankets, bags, and more!

üß∂ Materials

  • Worsted weight yarn (in one or more colors)
  • 5.0 mm (H-8) crochet hook
  • Scissors and yarn needle

üìù Pattern Instructions (US Terms)

  1. Start: Begin with a magic ring OR chain 4 and slip stitch to form a ring.
  2. Round 1: Ch 3 (counts as 1 dc), 2 dc in ring, ch 2. *(3 dc, ch 2)* three more times. Join with sl st to top of beginning ch-3. (4 clusters)
  3. Round 2: Sl st to next ch-2 space. Ch 3, 2 dc, ch 2, 3 dc in same space (corner). Repeat in each ch-2 space. Join with sl st.
  4. Round 3: Work *(3 dc) in each side space, and (3 dc, ch 2, 3 dc) in each corner*. Join with sl st.
  5. Round 4+: Continue pattern, adding side clusters and corner clusters as needed.

üí° Helpful Tips

  • Change colors after any round for a multi-color effect.
  • Use a stitch marker to keep track of rounds if needed.
  • Block squares after finishing for uniform size and shape.
üåü Bonus: Join finished squares using slip stitch, whip stitch, or join-as-you-go methods for larger projects like blankets or scarves.
